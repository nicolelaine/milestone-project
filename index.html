Projects

Maptime Berlin is quarterly event of map enthusiasts and mapping professionals alike. We offer workshops, have interesting lectures, and we occasionally go on an outting to a map library or exhibtion. I've been a co-organizer since 2017. If you'd like to speak at one of our events (post COVID times, of course!) please do get in touch! We welcome anyone who has a map related project that they would like to present, and we are especially beginner friendly. You can find our Meetup page here.

Missing Maps is an OpenStreetMap project that works in conjuction with the Red Cross to help put vulnerable communities on the map. We work via large scale mapathons and other humanitarian effors to ensure that people who need disaster relief are able to get the help they need as quickly as possible. We help map communties that have experienced or will soon experience hurricanes, floods, or other natural disasters, and we are especially focued right now on vaccination stations and food aid. You can find a link to the project here.

"Map your Desire Path" is a collaborate project to put the Desire Paths you see in the world around you onto the map. A Desire Path is a path or way that forms when pedestrian and foot traffic wears away at the natural or urban landscape, often in a way that in contrary to land and urban planning. Desire Paths can show where the people "voted with their feet" and can be helpful in planning future cities. This project aims to help people notice the world around us, and to take a "people first" approach to the places where we live.
